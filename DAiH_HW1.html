b) Provide an interpretation of each coefficient in the model. Be carefulsome of the variables in the model are qualitative!

  • Price : The coefficient value -0.054459 indicates that for every $1 increase in car seat price there may be an average estimated effect of 54.459 fewer unit sales at stores with all other variables being fixed.1
  • Urban : The coefficient value -0.021916 indicates that when stores are located in urban areas there may be an average estimated effect of 21.916 fewer unit sales than stores located in rural areas with all other variables being fixed.
  • US : The coefficient value 1.200573 indicates that when stores are located in the United States there may be an average estimated effect of 1200.573 more unit sales than stores located outside of the United States with all other variables being fixed.

c) Write out the model in equation form, being careful to handle the qualitative variables properly.

Based on the equation form \(\hat{Y} = \hat\beta_0 + \hat\beta_1X_1 + \hat\beta_2X_2 + \hat\beta_3X_3 + \epsilon\), then the model equation is

\[ Sales = 13.043469 - 0.054459(X_{Price}) - 0.021916(X_{Urban}) + 1.200573(X_{US}) + \epsilon\]

where \(X_{Urban} = 1\) if the store is in an urban location, \(X_{Urban} = 0\) if the store is in a rural location, \(X_{US} = 1\) if the store is located in the US, and \(X_{US} = 0\) if the store is located outside the US.

h) Is there evidence of outliers or high leverage observations in the model from part (e)?

Checking for outliers requires seeing if the model produces studentized residual values that are greater than 3 or less than -3. Checking for high leverage observations requires comparing observation leverage values to the average leverage value, which is uqual to \((p + 1)/n\).

#Displaying residual plots to get a visualization of potential outliers 
#and potential high leverage observations
library(ggplot2)
library(ggfortify)
plots = autoplot(csReg2, colour = rgb(0.8,0.1,0.3,0.6),
         smooth.colour = 'blue', smooth.linetype = 'dashed',
         ad.color = rgb(0.8,0.1,0.3,0.6),
         label.size = 3, label.n = 5, label.colour = 'black')
plots

studR = sort(rstudent(csReg2))
studR[studR > 3 | studR < (-3)] #check studentized residuals
## named numeric(0)

All studentized residual values are less than 3 and greater than -3, so there is not evidence of meaningful outliers.

avgLev = (2 + 1)/nrow(Carseats)

CD = sort(cooks.distance(csReg2)) 
CD[CD > 1]
## named numeric(0)

There are 6 observations with leverage values that are more than 3 times larger than the average leverage,2 so that may be considered evidence that there are high leverage observations relative to the rest of the observations. However, looking at the Cooks distance of each observation shows that none of the observations were highly influential since the Cooks distance values were all less than 1.
